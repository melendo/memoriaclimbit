\chapter*{Abstract}

\section*{\tituloPortadaEngVal}

Progress in an individual sport like climbing is often slow and marked by long plateaus, which pose a significant challenge in maintaining a climber's motivation and consistency. Adding to this problem is the fact that tracking progress and achievements is done manually in most cases, relying on memory or physical notebooks. 

The main objective of this work is to solve these two problems through a mobile application that digitizes route management in climbing gyms and provides climbers with comprehensive tracking of their activity. To tackle the problem of demotivation, the tool incorporates gamification mechanics based on the PBL (Points, Badges, and Leaderboards) system, whose elements transform the training routine into a more dynamic experience, allowing users to visualize their progress and encouraging healthy competition that reinforces a sense of belonging to the climbing community. 

The project has been developed under a UCD (User-Centered Design) approach, ensuring that the requirement definition, improvement proposals, and testing stem directly from the actual needs of the climbers. In this way, the built solution provides real value and adapts to the needs of the end users.
